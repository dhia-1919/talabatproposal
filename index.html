ADDRESSING THE QUESTION

Why a one-time fee doesn't work

This is the most common question we receive on AI pricing. Here's a transparent breakdown of why recurring pricing is the only model that protects both parties.

01
The AI runs on dedicated GPU infrastructure — 24/7
AI visual compliance requires dedicated high-performance GPU compute running continuously. This infrastructure has a fixed monthly cost whether it processes zero images or three million. A one-time fee cannot cover an ongoing hardware commitment.
02
Your gear standards aren't static — the AI can't be either
Talabat changes uniforms, introduces new branding, rolls out seasonal gear, adjusts safety requirements across markets. A one-time model is frozen at delivery. Within 6 months it scores against outdated standards. Ongoing pricing covers continuous retraining across all 8 markets.
03
AI accuracy degrades without active monitoring
Image quality varies by device, lighting, and rider behavior. Without continuous monitoring, false positive and negative rates drift upward. The service includes real-time accuracy tracking, weekly precision reporting, and automatic model correction.
04
Building internally is recurring cost too
Internal build means developer salaries (recurring), GPU compute (recurring), cloud infrastructure (recurring), and maintenance (recurring). The choice isn't one-time vs. recurring — it's your recurring cost vs. ours. Ours is lower because infrastructure is shared across clients.
05
Per-check pricing is lower risk for you
Costs scale proportionally. Low-usage month? Lower cost. Pause a market? No charges. Scale to new countries? Predictable growth. You never overpay for unused capacity.
